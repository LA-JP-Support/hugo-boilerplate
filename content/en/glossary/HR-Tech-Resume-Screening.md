## What is HR Tech Resume Screening?

**HR Tech Resume Screening is technology that uses AI and [natural language processing](Natural-Language-Processing.md) to automatically analyze resumes from large numbers of applicants and score fit against position requirements.** It dramatically reduces manual work time for recruiters and improves recruitment efficiency.

> **In a nutshell:** Like how email filters automatically judge spam—HR Tech Resume Screening automatically evaluates resumes, quickly extracting top candidates.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Automatically parse applicant resumes and score compatibility with position requirements
- **Why it's needed:** Large companies face thousands of applications; humans need immense time to review all
- **Who uses it:** High-volume recruiters in large companies, startup hiring managers

## Why it matters

The recruitment market sees surging applicant numbers. Hundreds of applications for single positions are now common. Manually reviewing all is impossible, risking excellent candidates being overlooked.

Simultaneously, relying on manual work introduces bias and errors. Unconscious biases like "favoring prestigious universities" easily occur. AI screening evaluates candidates fairly and quickly.

## How it works

HR Tech Resume Screening operates through three steps.

**The first is text extraction.** Extract text information from resumes in PDF or image format.

**The second is information structuring.** Using [natural language processing](Natural-Language-Processing.md), automatically recognize name, experience, skills, education, etc., converting to structured data.

**The third is matching evaluation.** Compare position requirements ("Python 3+ years," "Sales experience 3 years") with candidate skills. Calculate scores.

For example, for a web engineer position with requirements "Python," "AWS," "5+ years experience," the system automatically scores matching resumes high, reducing recruiter-reviewed resumes from 100 to 20.

## Real-world use cases

**Initial screening for high application volume**

When job openings receive many applications, AI performs initial filtering. Recruiters conduct interviews with top candidates only.

**Skill matching accuracy**

From resumes stating "Python experience," automatically determine actual years and specific project experience. Reduces hiring mismatches.

**Recruitment branding**

As application numbers and quality improve, "selective hiring" creates positive brand image.

## Benefits and considerations

**Benefits** include major recruiter workload reduction, speed improvement (initial screening 10x faster), bias mitigation (mechanical evaluation). Accumulated data enables analyzing successful hiring trends.

**Considerations** include AI model training data containing bias risk—such bias may replicate. Also risks filtering out uniquely qualified candidates (career changers) who don't fit requirements.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: How accurate is resume screening AI?**

A: Generally 85-95%. Accuracy depends on training data quality and requirement clarity. Regular tuning is important.

**Q: Can startups use it?**

A: Yes. Cloud-based services at reasonable prices are available for small-scale deployment.

**Q: Is candidate privacy protected?**

A: Select trusted vendors and specify information protection in contracts. GDPR and personal data protection law compliance is essential.
