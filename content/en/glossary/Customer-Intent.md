## What is Customer Intent?

**Customer intent refers to the underlying intention or purpose behind a customer's actions.** Search keywords, page duration, and click patterns reveal purchase intent and research motivation, enabling timely, relevant outreach.

> **In a nutshell:** Understanding the "real purpose" behind customer actions. When you deliver exactly what they need at the right moment, success rates soar.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Analyzes search and click behavior to determine "what the customer wants right now."
- **Why it matters:** Delivering the right message at the right time dramatically increases purchase likelihood.
- **Who uses it:** Marketing, sales, and customer service teams use intent insights to improve customer interactions.

## How it works

Customer intent falls into four main categories. **Purchase intent** involves keywords like "product + buy," indicating decision-ready customers. **Comparison intent** shows customers evaluating multiple options with keywords like "product A vs. product B." **Information-seeking intent** includes keywords like "how to use" or "reviews," indicating learning goals. **Navigation intent** describes customers directly visiting specific brand sites.

Machine learning can go beyond keywords to incorporate past behavior patterns. The same keyword "price" means different things to a customer accustomed to premium products versus one prioritizing budget. Automatically identifying this difference and delivering personalized messages can improve conversion rates by 30-50%.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: How reliable is intent recognition accuracy?**
A: Single signals (like a keyword) achieve 50-60% accuracy, but combining multiple signals reaches 85%+ accuracy.

**Q: Is real-time intent recognition technically feasible?**
A: Yes. Modern marketing automation platforms enable response to customer behavior within seconds.

**Q: Can intent analysis apply to both B2C and B2B?**
A: Yes, though B2B sales requires more complex analysis due to multiple decision-makers.
