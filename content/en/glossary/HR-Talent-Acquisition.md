## What is HR Talent Acquisition?

**HR Talent Acquisition is an approach that strategically designs and executes the recruitment process from hiring through onboarding, efficiently securing high-quality talent.** It systematizes recruitment channels, screening, interviews, and offers.

> **In a nutshell:** Like how vending machines have limited sales, but expanding through a sales strategy multiplies customers—recruitment also requires strategic deployment.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Strategically plan and execute overall recruitment to secure optimal talent
- **Why it's needed:** Securing excellent talent is key to company growth. Recruitment failures cause major losses
- **Who uses it:** Recruitment managers, HR departments, leadership

## Why it matters

Recruitment is the largest investment a company makes. Considering post-hiring training costs, early departure losses, and productivity gaps, recruitment quality greatly impacts the entire organization. Acquiring and retaining excellent talent creates competitive advantage.

Conversely, in an era of declining population and skill shortages, without efficient recruitment strategy, securing necessary talent becomes difficult.

## How it works

Talent acquisition proceeds through four steps.

**The first is recruitment planning.** Based on business strategy, clarify "how many people with what skills are needed." 3-5 year medium-term planning beyond annual goals is important.

**The second is sourcing (candidate discovery).** Gather candidates through multiple channels: job boards, recruiters, social media, referrals, etc. Analyze effectiveness by channel and optimize.

**The third is screening.** Select candidates through resume review, simple tests, interviews. More companies use AI to reduce interviewer burden.

**The fourth is interviews through onboarding.** Proceed through multiple interviews, make offers, and handle hiring paperwork and training.

As a real example, a startup expanded from 3 to 5 recruitment channels and measured each channel's effectiveness. Quality applicants increased 30%.

## Real-world use cases

**Large-scale recruiting for growth-stage companies**

Secure large volumes of people needed during scale-up while maintaining quality. Standardizing recruitment process is key.

**Role-specific recruitment approaches**

Set different screening standards for engineers versus sales. Create optimal recruitment schedules for each position.

**Diversity recruitment**

Design recruitment process to prevent unconscious bias. Training interviewers is important.

## Benefits and considerations

**Benefits** include improved recruitment quality, shortened recruitment periods (earlier staffing), recruitment cost reduction. "Transparent recruitment standards" reduce unfairness perceptions.

**Considerations** include intensifying recruitment competition; top talent is fiercely contested. Lengthy recruitment processes cause candidates to go elsewhere. Pressure for quick results is also a challenge.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: How long does recruitment take?**

A: Typically 3-6 months by role. Highly competitive roles like engineers can exceed six months.

**Q: What's the recruitment cost per hire?**

A: Roughly 300,000-1,000,000 yen for entry-level positions, 1,500,000-3,000,000 yen for managers. Varies with job boards and headhunting.

**Q: What should we do if recruitment fails?**

A: Detect early and consider appropriate follow-up or role changes. If still unsuitable, validate and improve recruitment decisions.
