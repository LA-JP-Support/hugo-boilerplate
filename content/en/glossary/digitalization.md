## What is Digitalization?

**Digitalization converts analog information and processes into digital form, then leverages that to transform operations and create new business value.** Unlike digitization (just converting to digital), digitalization uses converted data to fundamentally change how organizations operate.

> **In a nutshell:** "Scanning a paper document to PDF" is digitization. "Integrating that PDF into automated workflows for automatic approval" is digitalization.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Uses digitized data and technology to automate processes, speed decisions, and improve customer experience
- **Why it matters:** Manual work reduction, quality improvement, speed increase drive competitive strength
- **Who uses it:** Every organization seeking efficiency uses digitalization

## Why it matters

Digitalization isn't just efficiency—it's strategic. Business speed accelerates. Customer expectations rise. Paper-based slow processes can't compete.

Digitalization lets organizations make instant information access decisions, quickly identify bottlenecks, and rapidly respond to customer needs. Example: insurance companies who digitalize claims go from 3-week to 3-day processing while maintaining quality and reducing costs. Banks delete branch offices while improving customer convenience. True digitalization value.

## How it works

Digitalization happens in three steps.

First, convert existing analog processes to digital form: scanning documents, entering manual data to databases, shifting email-based approvals to workflow systems.

Second, use digitized data for automation and optimization. Smart routing based on authority, anomaly detection triggering alerts, ML-based smart decisions.

Third, create new business opportunities. Customer behavior data informs new product development; real-time insight creates new service models.

Digitalization success requires not just technology but organizational awareness change and skill upgrading. Simple system replacement without process innovation doesn't unlock value.

## Real-world use cases

**HR digitalization**

Hiring to payroll previously crossed systems and spreadsheets. Cloud-integrated HR system digitalization puts everything in one place. Onboarding shrinks from days to hours. Payroll fully automates. HR focuses on strategic talent development.

**Accounting automation**

Invoice processing auto-scans, digitizes, routes by authority. From receipt to posting happens without human touch. Monthly close shrinks from month-long to one-week. Real-time financial visibility.

**Customer service digitalization**

Multi-channel (phone, email, chat, social) customer issues funnel into one platform. AI handles initial response. Complex issues auto-escalate to humans. Satisfaction improves and response speeds skyrocket.

## Benefits and considerations

Digitalization's benefits are clear: manual work reduction, error decrease, speed increase, cost reduction. Real-time information improves decision quality. Customer service quality improves.

However, existing processes have strong organizational attachment/dependency—resistance is real. Legacy system integration is technically complex; migration risks are high. Digital platforms attract cyber attacks; security is mandatory. Skill gaps require additional training investment.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: What's digitization vs. digitalization?**

A: Digitization is "convert PDF from paper." Digitalization is "integrate PDF into automated workflows." Digitalization creates business value; digitization just converts format. Digitalization matters more.

**Q: Can digitalization really reduce costs?**

A: Yes. Initial investment is needed but long-term manual work elimination, process optimization, and error reduction save money. Initial chaos can temporarily increase costs without planning.

**Q: Can small companies digitalize?**

A: Absolutely. Cloud services eliminate large upfront investment. Small enterprises can operate efficiently digitally. Organization awareness and process redesign are mandatory.
