## What is Next Best Action?

**Next Best Action (NBA) is an AI technology that uses machine learning and predictive analytics to automatically determine the optimal next action (suggestion or message) for customers.** It analyzes customer behavior history, attributes, and real-time context to recommend the action predicted to have the highest impact at that moment.

> **In a nutshell:** An AI system that learns a customer's preferences and presents them with perfectly tailored offers at the optimal time.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** An AI system that predicts and recommends individually optimized actions based on customer data
- **Why it matters:** Enables individual optimization of customer experience at scale and precision impossible with manual work
- **Who uses it:** E-commerce companies, financial institutions, contact centers, large retailers

## Why it matters

Traditional marketing used segment-level approaches applying identical tactics to groups. NBA can individually determine the optimal action for millions of customers at each moment. This simultaneously improves customer satisfaction and sales. For example, if a customer frequently browses a specific product category, timely offers of related products significantly increase purchase rates. NBA can also detect customers likely to churn and proactively propose appropriate incentives.

## How it works

NBA systems aggregate information from multiple data sources—purchase history, website behavior, customer service records, and external factors like time and season. Machine learning models are applied to this data to predict the optimal action for each customer.

The system automatically selects the action expected to deliver the highest impact from multiple candidates such as "send email," "offer discount," or "suggest cross-sell," for each customer. Afterward, actual results become feedback to improve the model's predictive accuracy. This achieves individual optimization at a scale and precision impossible for humans.

## Real-world use cases

**Telecom upselling** — Predict data usage from calling patterns and automatically present optimal plan upgrade suggestions.

**Bank cross-selling** — Optimize timing of loan or investment product recommendations based on customer savings and life stage.

**Retail inventory management** — Display recommended products based on past purchases and predict restocking timing.

## Benefits and considerations

**Benefits** — Improve both customer satisfaction and sales; make faster, higher-confidence decisions than manual judgment. Providing one-on-one service to massive customer bases is uniquely possible with NBA.

**Considerations** — Poor data quality reduces accuracy. Customers may find repeated similar recommendations tedious. Privacy concerns must be addressed transparently.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: What's the difference between NBA and traditional segmentation?**
A: Segmentation groups customers and delivers identical tactics. NBA proposes different actions to individual customers—fundamentally different.

**Q: How long does implementation take?**
A: Basic implementation takes 3-6 months, but continuous improvement is needed for accuracy gains.

**Q: Can small businesses adopt NBA?**
A: Cloud-based SaaS NBA services enable adoption by small businesses. However, a certain volume of customer data is necessary.
