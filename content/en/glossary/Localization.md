## What is Localization?

**Localization is the process of adapting products, services, and content to the language, culture, and regulatory requirements of a target market.** It includes far more than translation alone—it encompasses user interface adjustments, payment method adaptation, and legal compliance. Building on the foundation of [internationalization (i18n)](Internationalization.md), detailed regional customization delivers a native experience for users.

> **In a nutshell:** It's like adjusting a television made for America so it also works in Japan.

**Key points:**
- **What it does:** Adapts products to local markets in each region
- **Why it's needed:** Competitiveness in global markets and improved user satisfaction
- **Who uses it:** Software companies, [global e-commerce](E-commerce.md), [SaaS](SaaS.md) companies

## Why it matters

In global markets, language and cultural adaptation significantly impact sales and customer satisfaction. Inaccurate translation or cultural inappropriateness damages brand image and causes market penetration to fail. Conversely, systematic localization delivers authentic user experiences in each region, leading to **higher user adoption**, **increased customer loyalty**, and **stronger market competitiveness**.

## How it works

Localization proceeds through five major steps.

First, perform **content extraction and analysis** to identify text, images, and other media for translation. Next, leverage [TMS (Translation Management System)](Translation-Management-System.md) to control work across multiple translators and ensure unified quality. Then, implement **cultural adaptation**—swapping images, adjusting color schemes, and redesigning UX to match regional preferences. Finally, conduct [quality assurance](QA.md) testing to verify each language version functions properly, then deploy to production.

This is the standard workflow that multinational enterprise product teams execute daily.

## Real-world use cases

**Mobile app global launch**
App development companies expanding simultaneously to Japan, France, Brazil, and other countries adapt UI text, navigation, and region-specific features (such as payment methods) for each language.

**SaaS platform multilingual support**
Cloud service companies provide user dashboards, documentation, and customer support in multiple languages, increasing customer satisfaction in each region.

**Game global launch**
Game developers localize story, characters, UI, and region-specific content, maximizing sales in each market.

## Benefits and considerations

**Benefits** include improved market penetration, higher user satisfaction, and stronger brand credibility. **Considerations** include the complex management structure required and the specialized knowledge needed to avoid cultural misunderstandings.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: What's the difference between localization and translation?**
A: Translation is language conversion alone, but localization additionally includes cultural adaptation and technical considerations.

**Q: Do we need to support all countries?**
A: No. It's more efficient to prioritize important markets first. Identifying target markets through data analysis is important.

**Q: How much does localization cost?**
A: It varies significantly based on the number of languages, content volume, and quality requirements. It's important to estimate scope upfront.
