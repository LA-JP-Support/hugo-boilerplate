## What is Google?

**Google is a leading global technology company that began with a search engine and now operates diversified businesses including AI, cloud, advertising, and hardware.** Under parent company Alphabet Inc., it also leads the industry in AI research and implementation.

> **In a nutshell:** A company that started with the mission to "organize the world's information and make it universally accessible" is now transforming entire industries through AI and cloud.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Technology company providing search, advertising, cloud services, and AI development
- **Why it matters:** Search quality significantly impacts business operations, and AI development leads cutting-edge technology
- **Who uses it:** Individuals through enterprises and government agencies

## Major products and services

**Search and Google business**

Google's core [search](Google-Search.md) business is the world's largest search engine used by billions monthly. Google Adsense advertising networks generate about 80% of revenue, functioning as the marketing foundation for companies.

**Google Cloud Platform**

Cloud services including [Vertex AI](Vertex-AI.md) accelerate enterprise AI adoption. With enterprise-grade security and scalability, major corporations and government agencies worldwide use them.

**Gemini AI**

A [multimodal](Multimodal.md) AI model understanding text, images, audio, and video. It's used across applications from chatbots to complex enterprise task automation. [Prompt engineering](Prompt-Engineering.md) advances enable more precise control.

**Gmail, Google Workspace**

Email and business collaboration tools. Individuals and companies worldwide use them daily, forming the core of [cloud](Cloud.md)-based productivity improvements.

## Competition and alternatives

**[Amazon Web Services (AWS)](AWS.md)**

Cloud infrastructure leader. AWS has greater market share than Google and offers more service options. For AI development, it provides [SageMaker](SageMaker.md), directly competing with Google Cloud.

**[Microsoft Azure](Microsoft-Azure.md)**

Integration with Office 365 is capturing enterprise users. Strong investment in [generative AI](Generative-AI.md) like Copilot threatens Google in enterprise markets.

**[OpenAI](OpenAI.md) / ChatGPT**

High consumer awareness of [large language models](Large-Language-Model.md), with suggestions it could replace Google Search.

## Google's strategic position

Google generates enormous profits from search advertising and invests that revenue in AI research. From basic research via Google DeepMind to practical applications like Vertex AI, it pursues consistent AI strategy with major industry impact.

However, stronger EU regulations ([GDPR](GDPR.md), Digital Markets Act) and technology competition with China pose challenges. Meanwhile, massive [data center](Data-Center.md) investments continue strengthening global infrastructure.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: What's the difference between Google and Alphabet?**

A: Alphabet is the parent company, and Google LLC is its subsidiary. Google LLC operates search, advertising, and cloud services, while Alphabet also holds other companies (Waymo, Verily, Calico, etc.).

**Q: How much profit does Google make?**

A: Annual operating profit exceeds $50 billion, almost entirely from advertising business. It invests over $40 billion annually in AI development.

**Q: Does Google have successful businesses besides search?**

A: Google Cloud is expanding at high growth rates. YouTube advertising, Google Workspace, and hardware like Pixel are also becoming important revenue sources.
