## What is SEO?

**SEO involves improving website content and technical structure so it ranks higher on search engines like Google, growing traffic without paid ads.** When users search for "phone repair," the goal is for them to naturally (organically) discover your site rather than through paid ads. Success depends on search engines judging "this site is trustworthy and answers user questions."

> **In a nutshell:** Getting search engines to recognize "your website has the answers users are searching for."

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Improve content, technology, and links to increase organic search visibility
- **Why it matters:** Acquire traffic and customers continuously without relying on ad budgets
- **Who uses it:** [E-commerce companies](Search-Analytics.md), media, SaaS, all online businesses

## Why it matters

While monthly Google Ads costs tens of thousands, top SEO ranking generates traffic free forever after. Websites ranking on search's first page enjoy higher user trust and tend to convert better than paid ads. Further, by creating content based on [search intent](Search-Intent.md), you don't just drive traffic—you attract "genuinely interested customers," which is extremely important.

## How it works

SEO has three major pillars.

**On-page SEO** is content optimization itself. Place target keywords naturally in titles, headings, and body, providing quality information so Google judges "this page is authoritative on this topic."

**Technical SEO** is website structure. Slow page load speeds or poor mobile display prevent Google's crawler from efficiently exploring your site. XML sitemap configuration, robots.txt setup, and schema markup implementation help search engines understand your site accurately.

**Link building** collects external "votes." Links from trusted websites signal to Google that your site is trustworthy and deserves higher ranking. Quality matters—irrelevant massive links backfire.

## Real-world use cases

**Local SEO for regional business** Dentists appearing at the top for "Shibuya dentist" get many new patients monthly. Google Business Profile optimization is critical.

**Customer discovery through blogs** Blog articles ranking high for "WordPress getting started" generated signup increases for WordPress hosting services.

**Product page optimization** Rewriting existing product pages to match [search intent](Search-Intent.md) improved click-through rate 40% and increased sales.

## Benefits and considerations

**Benefits** are long-term stable traffic acquisition. Unlike paid ads, rankings persist when budget ends. You compete on content quality rather than getting caught in price wars with competitors.

**Considerations** include 3-6 months for results. [Algorithm updates](Search-Optimization.md) can suddenly drop rankings. For short-term results, combining with paid advertising works best.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Is SEO alone sufficient marketing strategy?**
A: SEO is important but combining [paid ads](Search-Analytics.md), social, and email marketing is more effective. New businesses especially need paid tactics to compensate while SEO results develop.

**Q: How much budget does SEO specifically require?**
A: In-house efforts can start for tens of thousands monthly. Outsourcing to agencies typically costs 100,000-500,000 monthly. Key is consistency—steady monthly investment trumps one-time spending.
