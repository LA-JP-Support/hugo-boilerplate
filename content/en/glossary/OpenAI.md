## What is OpenAI?

OpenAI is a leading artificial intelligence (AI) company focused on building and deploying advanced AI models for public benefit. Founded in 2015, OpenAI has developed some of the world's most advanced large language models (LLMs), generative image systems, and AI-powered tools that have transformed how people and businesses interact with artificial intelligence. OpenAI's mission is to ensure that artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity, and its products are widely used for automation, content creation, software development, customer service, and scientific research.

<strong>Key Products and Services:</strong>- <strong>ChatGPT</strong>- Advanced conversational AI assistant powered by GPT models for natural language interactions, content creation, coding assistance, and problem-solving across diverse domains
- <strong>GPT Model Series</strong>- State-of-the-art large language models (GPT-3.5, GPT-4, GPT-5 series) providing the foundation for ChatGPT and API services with industry-leading reasoning and generation capabilities
- <strong>DALL-E 3</strong>- AI image generation system creating detailed, creative images from text descriptions
- <strong>Codex</strong>- Code generation and analysis system powering GitHub Copilot and enabling natural language to code translation
- <strong>Whisper</strong>- Automatic speech recognition system for transcription and translation across dozens of languages
- <strong>OpenAI API</strong>- Developer platform providing programmatic access to all OpenAI models for custom applications and integrations

<strong>Related Product Articles:</strong>For detailed information about OpenAI's flagship conversational assistant, see ChatGPT. For comprehensive technical details about the underlying model architecture and capabilities, see GPT.

## Company Background

OpenAI was founded in December 2015 in San Francisco by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, John Schulman, and Wojciech Zaremba. The founders created OpenAI as non-profit with intention of freely sharing research to avoid risks of proprietary AGI. In 2019, OpenAI transitioned to "capped-profit" model, establishing OpenAI LP (for-profit entity) controlled by original OpenAI non-profit to allow investment and commercial scaling while maintaining mission alignment.

<strong>Key Events:</strong>- <strong>2015:</strong>OpenAI launched with $1 billion in funding commitments
- <strong>2019:</strong>Transitions to capped-profit model, receives $1 billion investment from Microsoft, partners for exclusive cloud infrastructure via Azure
- <strong>2023-2025:</strong>Revenue grows rapidly, surpassing $13B annualized by mid-2025, driven by widespread adoption of API and corporate partnerships
- <strong>2024:</strong>Apple signs contract to integrate ChatGPT into Apple Intelligence
- <strong>August 2025:</strong>Launch of GPT-5, unified system with intelligent routing
- <strong>November 2025:</strong>Launch of GPT-5.1 with improved conversational abilities
- <strong>December 2025:</strong>Launch of GPT-5.2, most advanced model for professional work

## Mission and Objectives

OpenAI's core mission is to ensure that AGI—highly autonomous systems that outperform humans at most economically valuable work—benefits all of humanity.

<strong>Objectives:</strong>

<strong>Advance AI capabilities:</strong>Develop increasingly powerful and general AI systems.

<strong>Safety and ethics:</strong>Prioritize safe deployment and research on AI alignment.

<strong>Transparency:</strong>Share research with public to foster openness and understanding.

<strong>Democratization:</strong>Make advanced AI broadly accessible and useful.

<strong>Human values alignment:</strong>Ensure AI systems reflect human intentions and ethical standards.

## Major Products

### Large Language Models (LLMs)

<strong>GPT-5 Series (Latest Generation):</strong>

<strong>GPT-5.2 (December 2025):</strong>Most advanced model for professional knowledge work. Available in three versions:
- <strong>GPT-5.2 Instant:</strong>Speed-optimized for routine queries like information-seeking, writing, and translation
- <strong>GPT-5.2 Thinking:</strong>Excels at complex structured work including coding, analyzing long documents, math, and planning
- <strong>GPT-5.2 Pro:</strong>Maximum accuracy and reliability for difficult problems, with extended reasoning capabilities

<strong>Key GPT-5.2 Capabilities:</strong>- Expert-level performance on professional tasks across 44 occupations (GDPval benchmark: 70.9% match or exceed expert performance)
- State-of-the-art coding, math, and science capabilities (GPQA Diamond: 93.2%, FrontierMath: 40.3%)
- 80% fewer hallucinations compared to previous models
- 38% fewer errors in Thinking mode responses
- Superior long-context understanding (272K input tokens, 128K output tokens)
- Advanced vision, tool-calling, and agentic workflow capabilities
- Integrated into GitHub Copilot for enhanced code generation

<strong>GPT-5.1 (November 2025):</strong>Improved conversational model with warmer personality, better suited for agentic and coding tasks.

<strong>GPT-5 (August 2025):</strong>Unified system with intelligent routing between fast default model and deeper "Thinking" mode for complex problems.

<strong>Earlier GPT Models:</strong>

<strong>GPT-4o (Omni):</strong>Released in 2024, processes text, audio, and vision in real time with improved speed and capability.

<strong>GPT-4:</strong>Multimodal model accepting image and text inputs, producing text outputs.

<strong>GPT-3.5 Turbo:</strong>Widely used for conversational AI and text generation.

<strong>ChatGPT:</strong>Conversational AI built on GPT models, available on web, mobile, and API. Supports multi-modal input/output and advanced plugin integrations. Now features intelligent routing to use appropriate GPT-5 variant based on task complexity.

### DALL-E

<strong>DALL·E 3:</strong>AI system for generating images from text descriptions. Supports detailed, creative image generation, available to all ChatGPT users and developers via API.

### Codex

<strong>Codex:</strong>AI system for code generation, interpretation, and explanation in multiple programming languages. Powers GitHub Copilot (now enhanced with GPT-5.2) and supports integration in IDEs, Slack, and other developer tools.

### Whisper

<strong>Whisper:</strong>Automatic speech recognition (ASR) system trained on 680,000 hours of multilingual and multitask supervised data. Supports transcription and translation across dozens of languages, available via API.

### OpenAI API

<strong>OpenAI API:</strong>Provides programmatic access to language, code, image, and speech models for developers. Supports text generation, chatbots, image creation, code analysis, and audio transcription.

<strong>GPT-5 API Pricing:</strong>- GPT-5: $1.25/1M input tokens, $10/1M output tokens
- GPT-5 mini: $0.25/1M input tokens, $2/1M output tokens
- GPT-5 nano: $0.05/1M input tokens, $0.40/1M output tokens

### Other Tools

<strong>OpenAI Gym:</strong>Toolkit for developing and comparing reinforcement learning algorithms.

<strong>Assistants API:</strong>Enables creation of task-specific AI agents that can execute code, handle workflows, and integrate with external data.

## How It Works

### Training Data

OpenAI's models are trained on:
- Public web content (books, articles, websites)
- Licensed datasets
- Data generated or labeled by human trainers
- Excludes certain types of data (e.g., private user content, unless opted-in)

### Model Development

<strong>Machine Learning:</strong>Models learn statistical representations from massive datasets.

<strong>Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF):</strong>Human evaluators rank and refine outputs, guiding model behavior.

<strong>Neural Networks:</strong>Deep learning architectures (transformers) power LLMs, enabling complex pattern recognition across modalities.

<strong>Intelligent Routing (GPT-5 Series):</strong>Real-time router decides between fast model and deeper reasoning model based on conversation complexity and user intent.

### Abilities and Limitations

<strong>Models can:</strong>- Generate, summarize, and translate text
- Create images
- Write and interpret code with professional-grade quality
- Transcribe and translate speech
- Simulate interactive conversation
- Perform complex multi-step reasoning
- Execute long-running agentic workflows
- Analyze and understand long documents (up to 272K tokens)

<strong>Limitations:</strong>- No genuine understanding or consciousness
- Outputs are probabilistic, based on training data
- Cannot access real-time external data unless explicitly programmed
- May still produce errors despite significant improvements

### Deployment and Access

Web platforms (ChatGPT), mobile apps, and APIs. Enterprise solutions offer enhanced privacy, reliability, and administrative controls.

## Benefits and Limitations

### Benefits

<strong>Efficiency:</strong>Automates complex and repetitive tasks at professional-expert level.

<strong>Scalability:</strong>Operates at vast scale with consistent performance.

<strong>Personalization:</strong>Adapts responses and outputs to user context.

<strong>Creativity:</strong>Enables new content forms and ideation.

<strong>Insight Generation:</strong>Extracts actionable information from large datasets.

<strong>Language/Format Versatility:</strong>Works across languages, media, and industries.

<strong>Professional-Grade Quality:</strong>GPT-5.2 delivers outputs comparable to or exceeding expert professionals across many domains.

<strong>Cost Effectiveness:</strong>Delivers expert-level work at fraction of cost and time.

### Limitations

<strong>Not Perfect:</strong>While significantly improved, may still produce errors in some cases.

<strong>Bias:</strong>Reflects biases present in training data.

<strong>Transparency:</strong>"Black box" nature of deep learning models.

<strong>Data Privacy:</strong>Requires careful handling of sensitive or regulated data.

<strong>Cost:</strong>Enterprise-grade models and API usage can be expensive, though cost per task is decreasing.

<strong>Human Oversight:</strong>Outputs often need review, especially in regulated fields.

### Common Misconceptions

AI does <strong>not</strong>understand or reason like human; it predicts output based on patterns (though GPT-5.2 demonstrates expert-level reasoning in many domains).

AI is <strong>not always neutral</strong>; outputs can be biased.

AI augments human work; with GPT-5.2, this augmentation reaches expert-level assistance.

## Ethical Considerations and Governance

OpenAI emphasizes responsible AI development through governance, transparency, and collaboration.

<strong>Capped-Profit Structure:</strong>OpenAI LP allows controlled profit to attract funding, while non-profit retains mission control.

<strong>Board Oversight:</strong>OpenAI non-profit board has ultimate authority over OpenAI LP, including leadership changes and mission alignment.

<strong>Ethical Guidelines:</strong>Publishes usage policies, restricts high-risk applications, and regularly updates safety protocols.

<strong>Safety Testing:</strong>Comprehensive testing including disallowed content evaluations, jailbreak resistance, prompt injection defense, hallucination reduction, and deception mitigation.

<strong>Transparency:</strong>Research publications, model system cards, and capability disclosures.

<strong>Collaboration:</strong>Works with academia, industry, and policymakers to set standards.

### Ongoing Challenges

<strong>Copyright and Data Ownership:</strong>Lawsuits about training data (especially copyrighted material).

<strong>Bias and Fairness:</strong>Active research to mitigate bias and ensure fair outputs.

<strong>AI Alignment:</strong>Ensuring advanced AI follows human intentions reliably.

<strong>Safety at Scale:</strong>As models become more capable, ensuring safety and alignment becomes increasingly critical.

## Corporate Structure and Security Considerations

### Jurisdiction and Corporate Structure

<strong>Headquarters:</strong>San Francisco, California, United States

<strong>Legal Structure:</strong>OpenAI operates through a unique dual-entity structure designed to balance profit incentives with mission alignment:
- <strong>OpenAI Inc.</strong>- 501(c)(3) non-profit parent organization with ultimate control and fiduciary duty to humanity
- <strong>OpenAI LP</strong>- Capped-profit subsidiary allowing investor returns up to 100x while ensuring non-profit maintains governance control
- <strong>OpenAI Global LLC</strong>- Operating entity managing day-to-day business operations

This structure ensures profit motives remain subordinate to the mission of ensuring AGI benefits all humanity, with the non-profit board maintaining veto power over commercial decisions.

### Capital Structure and Major Investors

<strong>Total Funding:</strong>Over $13 billion raised through 2025

<strong>Major Investors:</strong>- <strong>Microsoft</strong>- $13 billion investment (largest investor), holds approximately 49% economic interest with governance protections preventing majority control
- <strong>Thrive Capital</strong>- Led recent funding rounds
- <strong>Khosla Ventures</strong>- Early stage investor
- <strong>Reid Hoffman</strong>- Individual investor and board member
- <strong>Additional investors</strong>- Various venture capital firms and strategic partners

<strong>Strategic Partnership:</strong>Microsoft provides exclusive cloud infrastructure through Azure, gaining preferential API access and integration rights while OpenAI maintains independent governance.

<strong>Investment Caps:</strong>Investor returns capped at 100x initial investment, with excess value flowing to non-profit to ensure mission alignment over profit maximization.

### Data Governance and Sovereignty

<strong>Data Center Locations:</strong>Primarily hosted on Microsoft Azure infrastructure with data centers in:
- United States (primary)
- European Union (GDPR compliance)
- Additional global regions through Azure network

<strong>Data Residency Options:</strong>- Enterprise customers can request regional data processing
- API data processed in Azure regions closest to customer
- Conversation data retained according to customer tier and preferences

<strong>Data Retention Policies:</strong>- Free tier: 30 days default retention for service improvement
- API/Enterprise: User data not used for training by default
- Users can delete data and opt out of training data usage
- Enterprise plans offer enhanced data isolation and retention controls

<strong>Government Data Access:</strong>- Subject to U.S. legal jurisdiction and CLOUD Act
- Responds to lawful government requests following established procedures
- Publishes transparency reports on government data requests
- Enterprise customers can implement additional encryption and access controls

### Regulatory Compliance and Certifications

<strong>Security Certifications:</strong>- <strong>SOC 2 Type II</strong>- Comprehensive security, availability, and confidentiality controls
- <strong>ISO 27001</strong>- Information security management systems (in progress)
- <strong>Enterprise-grade security</strong>- Encryption at rest and in transit, access controls, audit logging

<strong>Privacy Compliance:</strong>- <strong>GDPR</strong>(European Union) - Full compliance with data protection rights
- <strong>CCPA</strong>(California) - Consumer privacy rights implementation
- <strong>Privacy Shield alternatives</strong>- Standard Contractual Clauses for EU-US data transfers

<strong>Industry-Specific Compliance:</strong>- <strong>HIPAA</strong>- Available for healthcare customers through Business Associate Agreements
- <strong>FERPA</strong>- Education records protection available

<strong>AI-Specific Regulations:</strong>- Monitoring EU AI Act requirements for high-risk AI systems
- Participating in voluntary AI safety commitments with U.S. government
- Engaging with regulators globally on responsible AI development

### Geopolitical and Security Considerations

<strong>National Security Status:</strong>- U.S.-headquartered company subject to Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States (CFIUS) oversight
- Technology subject to U.S. export controls under International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) and Export Administration Regulations (EAR)
- Advanced AI capabilities considered dual-use technology with potential military applications

<strong>Export Restrictions:</strong>- GPT-4 and GPT-5 models subject to U.S. export controls for certain countries
- Service availability restricted in embargoed nations (Iran, North Korea, Syria, Cuba, etc.)
- API access requires verification for high-risk jurisdictions
- Chip export restrictions impact training infrastructure availability

<strong>Government Contracts:</strong>- Provides services to U.S. federal agencies and defense contractors
- Working with Pentagon on national security applications
- Participating in NIST AI Risk Management Framework development
- Collaborates with intelligence community on AI safety research

<strong>Five Eyes Alliance:</strong>- Operates within Five Eyes intelligence-sharing framework (US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand)
- Technology accessible to Five Eyes government agencies
- Subject to information sharing agreements between allied nations

<strong>Strategic Considerations for International Customers:</strong>- <strong>China:</strong>Service not directly available; technology export restricted
- <strong>Russia:</strong>Service restricted due to sanctions and export controls
- <strong>EU:</strong>Full service availability with GDPR compliance and data residency options
- <strong>Middle East:</strong>Available with considerations for regional data sovereignty requirements
- <strong>Asia-Pacific:</strong>Available in most markets with regional data processing options

<strong>Technology Transfer Controls:</strong>- Model weights considered controlled technology under U.S. regulations
- Training techniques subject to export restrictions
- Partnerships with foreign entities require government review
- Open-source releases evaluated for national security implications

### Enterprise Security Considerations

<strong>For Government and Regulated Industries:</strong>- Enhanced security features available through Enterprise plans
- Dedicated instances and private deployment options (limited availability)
- Custom data retention and deletion policies
- Advanced access controls and audit logging
- Incident response and security operations support

<strong>Supply Chain Security:</strong>- Dependency on Microsoft Azure infrastructure
- Hardware sourced primarily from U.S.-aligned suppliers
- Regular security audits of supply chain partners
- Monitoring for hardware vulnerabilities and backdoors

<strong>Recommendations for Risk-Sensitive Organizations:</strong>- Conduct independent security assessments before deployment
- Implement data classification and handling procedures
- Use private deployment options for sensitive workloads
- Maintain awareness of evolving regulatory requirements
- Establish incident response procedures for AI-related risks
- Consider data sovereignty requirements for international operations
