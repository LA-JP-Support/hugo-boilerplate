## What is Employee Advocacy?

**Employee advocacy is the practice of employees sharing company information and content through their personal social media accounts (LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.).** It is based on the principle that recommendations from real people are more trustworthy and relatable than official company communications.

> **In a nutshell:** A recommendation from a friend ("you should try this") is more convincing than a celebrity endorsement. When that friend is your employee, the effect multiplies.

**Key points:**

- **What it is:** Employees sharing company information on their personal social media
- **Why it matters:** More trustworthy than corporate messaging; reaches wider audiences
- **Who uses it:** B2B companies with active LinkedIn users, startups, and fast-growing companies

## Why it matters

Research shows that employee-shared content has 2-3 times higher engagement rates (clicks and shares) than LinkedIn ads. This is because the source is a "trusted individual," not a corporation.

From a hiring perspective, when employees share "this company is great," applications from quality candidates increase. This directly improves [employer brand](Employer-Brand.md).

## How it works

Three key elements drive successful employee advocacy. First, provide easily shareable content. Companies create blog posts, infographics, and resources that employees can quickly distribute without extra effort.

Second, establish clear guidelines. Clarify "what can be shared," "exclude trade secrets," and create a safe environment for participation. Transparency builds confidence.

Third, recognize and incentivize participation. Highlight top sharers in company communications, provide exclusive content access, and sustain ongoing engagement through appreciation.

## Real-world use cases

**SaaS Product Launch**

Sales teams post "our new feature solves this problem" on LinkedIn. Engagement reaches 10x the corporate account's typical metrics.

**Recruitment Campaign**

Multiple employees share "this company is truly great to work for" on social media. This authenticity attracts quality candidates and reduces hiring costs.

**Industry Conference Promotion**

Attending employees tweet "you should attend this conference." Industry professionals see the message more widely than official corporate announcements.

## Benefits and considerations

Key benefits include expanded reach, increased trust, and improved hiring outcomes. B2B and tech companies see especially high returns from LinkedIn-based employee advocacy.

Important consideration: participation must be voluntary. Forced sharing appears inauthentic and backfires. Over-monitoring participation also discourages involvement. Balance is essential.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Can we require employees to share on social media?**
A: Forced sharing loses authenticity. Keep participation voluntary, but clearly recognize and value those who participate.

**Q: What if an employee shares inappropriate content?**
A: Risks are generally low. Clear guidelines and quick response when issues arise are important. The company should monitor but not micromanage.

**Q: Does advocacy work for small companies?**
A: Limited employee numbers mean individual reach is constrained. However, quality content and relevance to target audiences can still produce results.
