## What is FlowHunt?

**FlowHunt is a no-code platform that enables AI chatbots and automation without programming.** Using drag-and-drop operations, you can build complex bots and workflows executing sophisticated AI tasks. It supports multiple AI models: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and others.

> **In a nutshell:** "Build AI systems like you'd create a PowerPoint presentation." Draw blocks, connect them—no code needed.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Design, deploy AI chatbots and workflows using a visual editor
- **Why it's needed:** Democratizes AI for companies without programmer resources
- **Who uses it:** Marketing teams, customer service departments, small and mid-size business users

## Why it matters

The [LLM](LLM.md) (Large Language Model) era made AI accessible to everyone. However, implementing complex flows typically requires programmers. FlowHunt and similar no-code tools let business users build themselves.

It handles customer service, lead generation, customer data analysis, and more. Deployment is simple. For companies saying "we want to use AI but lack development capacity," FlowHunt is transformative.

## How it works

FlowHunt has three layers. First, create a "flow" (automation sequence). Next, define "agents" (AI role assignments). Finally, connect "knowledge sources" (documents the AI references).

For a customer support bot: receive customer question → search FAQ knowledge base → generate optimal answer using [RAG](RAG.md) technique → return to customer. All built visually.

## Real-world use cases

**Customer support bot**
Embed on company website. Auto-answers questions, referencing [FAQ](FAQ.md) databases with latest info.

**Lead acquisition flow**
Auto-interview prospects at trade shows. Route warm leads to sales based on responses.

**Internal process automation**
Automate expense approvals, vacation requests, cutting manual work.

## Benefits and considerations

Benefits are huge. Development shrinks from months to weeks. Maintenance is easy—business teams iterate without burdening developers.

Limitations exist for complex logic: multiple conditional branches or intricate system integrations may still need programming.
