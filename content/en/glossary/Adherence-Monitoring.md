## What is Adherence Monitoring?

**Adherence Monitoring is technology that monitors and measures in real-time whether agents are working their scheduled shifts and handling customers as planned.** For example, if an agent is scheduled 9 AM-12 PM, the system verifies actual start/end times and identifies any unscheduled absences. Adherence Monitoring quantifies compliance rate (percentage of scheduled time worked) and drives improvement initiatives.

> **In a nutshell:** "Like school attendance tracking, but for contact center agents—verifying scheduled employees actually show up and work as planned."

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Monitors agent work versus scheduled shifts, measuring compliance
- **Why it matters:** Realizes benefits of [Workforce Scheduling](/en/glossary/Workforce-Scheduling/), shortens customer wait times
- **Who uses it:** Contact center managers, HR, executives

## Why it matters

Even perfect [Workforce Scheduling](/en/glossary/Workforce-Scheduling/) calculations fail if agents don't show up. If calculations indicate 10 agents needed Monday 10 AM but only 8 arrive, customer wait times increase.

Adherence Monitoring visualizes this "plan versus reality gap." Low compliance rates trigger root cause analysis—personal reasons? Unrealistic schedule? Results drive improvement. This maximizes [Workforce Scheduling](/en/glossary/Workforce-Scheduling/) ROI.

Adherence Monitoring also ensures fairness. Visible agent attendance reveals chronic tardiness or unauthorized absences, enabling targeted intervention.

## How it works

Adherence Monitoring integrates scheduling systems, timekeeping systems, and contact center ACDs (automatic call distributors).

**Stage 1: Schedule Definition** [Workforce Scheduling](/en/glossary/Workforce-Scheduling/) or management defines each agent's shift (start, breaks, end).

**Stage 2: Automatic Work Tracking** System automatically records agent login time, call start time, break time—all actions tracked like timecard entries.

**Stage 3: Real-time Comparison and Alerts** Adherence Monitoring continuously compares scheduled versus actual work. Discrepancies—like scheduled 10 AM call start but 10:05 AM actual start—trigger real-time manager alerts.

**Stage 4: Statistical Analysis and Improvement Planning** Accumulated data generates individual compliance scores: "Agent A 95% compliance, Agent B 78% compliance." These inform performance reviews and training plans.

## Real-world use cases

**Large contact center staffing optimization**
1,000+ agent company adopting Adherence Monitoring. Previously manual timecard management suffered from fraud and poor analysis. Automatic tracking visualized all agent attendance. Targeted support for low-compliance agents strengthened execution. Customer wait times decreased 12%.

**Agent satisfaction and compliance correlation**
Company analyzing Adherence Monitoring data plus employee satisfaction surveys found low-compliance agents expressed shift schedule dissatisfaction. More flexible shift policies increased compliance from 80% to 88% while reducing turnover.

**Remote work scheduling optimization**
Remote work expansion made traditional clock-in/clock-out verification impossible. Adherence Monitoring tracked login/logout time, VPN connection, system access—capturing genuine work while avoiding privacy-invasive surveillance like screenshots or mouse tracking.

## Benefits and considerations

**Benefits:**

Adherence Monitoring automates timecard management, eliminating fraud. Quantified compliance enables fairer performance evaluation. Low compliance root cause analysis enables appropriate responses—schedule adjustment, targeted support, training, or reassignment. [Workforce Scheduling](/en/glossary/Workforce-Scheduling/) execution improves, delivering planned benefits (wait time reduction, cost savings).

**Considerations:**

Excessive monitoring risks employee demoralization—"constantly watched" creates stress and turnover. Implementation requires clear communication of purpose (schedule reliability improvement, not punishment), flexible tolerance for brief legitimate absences (medical needs, hydration breaks), and human-centered operations.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: What compliance rate is acceptable?**
A: 95%+ is typical goal. 100% is unrealistic (system downtime, emergencies). Below 80% indicates systemic problems.

**Q: How do we handle low-compliance agents?**
A: First: root cause analysis. Is schedule unrealistic? Personal challenges? Skill stress? Then adjust shifts, increase support, provide training, or consider reassignment. Support comes first; consequences are last resort.

**Q: How does Adherence Monitoring work with remote workers?**
A: Track system login/logout, VPN connection, communication platform activity, call system status. Avoid excessive screenshots or activity monitoring—balance transparency with privacy.
