## What is Bot Containment Rate?

**Bot containment rate is a metric that measures the percentage of customer inquiries a chatbot can resolve completely without transferring to a human agent. For example, if a chatbot resolves 850 out of 1,000 inquiries, the containment rate is 85%. A higher rate indicates greater success in automating [customer service](Customer-Service.md).**

> **In a nutshell:** It's like a bank window where simple inquiries are handled by machine, and only complex requests go to staff. A higher ratio means greater efficiency.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Measures chatbot efficiency
- **Why it matters:** Quantifies the impact of automation on workforce reduction
- **Who uses it:** Contact center managers, customer service leaders

## Why it matters

High containment rates allow human agents to focus on complex inquiries, improving overall customer satisfaction. Additionally, it reduces customer support costs. Generally, a 70-90% containment rate is considered ideal. However, 100% should not be the goal—complex issues requiring human judgment should be appropriately escalated.

## How it works

Achieving high containment rates requires several components. First, [natural language processing](NLP.md) ([NLP](NLP.md)) technology is essential—accurately understanding user intent is prerequisite for appropriate responses. Second, a comprehensive [knowledge base](Knowledge-Base.md) is necessary. Without answers to expected questions in the database, escalation becomes unavoidable.

Integration with CRM (customer relationship management) systems is also important, allowing bots to access customer information and provide personalized responses.

## Benefits and advantages

**Cost reduction**
Reduced burden on human agents lowers hiring and training costs.

**Faster response**
Bots provide 24/7 support, eliminating wait times.

**Consistent quality**
Bots always provide the same quality answers, eliminating variation in agent performance.

**Scalability**
Multiple inquiries can be handled simultaneously.

## Real-world use cases

**Financial institution customer service**
Account balance inquiries and deposit confirmations are automated, achieving 90%+ containment rates.

**E-commerce order support**
Order status checks and return requests are handled by bots, freeing humans for complex complaints.

**Telecom technical support**
Initial troubleshooting is automated, with complex technical issues escalated to specialists.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: Is 100% containment rate possible?**
A: No, and it shouldn't be the goal. Complex issues requiring human judgment should be appropriately escalated.

**Q: What should we do to improve containment rate?**
A: Expand the [knowledge base](Knowledge-Base.md), improve [NLP](NLP.md) accuracy, and strengthen integration with backend systems.

**Q: If containment rate is low, is the bot useless?**
A: Not necessarily. Analyzing failure patterns and making improvements can significantly increase the rate.
