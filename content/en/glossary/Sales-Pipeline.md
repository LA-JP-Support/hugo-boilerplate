## What is Sales Pipeline?

**Sales Pipeline is a visual representation of all ongoing sales opportunities that sales teams classify by progressive stages from initial contact through closing, tracking and managing their progress.** Visualizing the process from initial awareness through closing enables sales managers to monitor pipeline health and understand expected revenue.

> **In a nutshell:** A way to organize and track which prospects a sales team is currently pursuing and where each prospect is in the buying process.

**Key points:**

- **What it does:** Classifies deals by stage within CRM, managing progress status, closing probability, and forecast amount
- **Why it's needed:** Enables managers to forecast revenue, optimize resource allocation, and intervene early on at-risk deals
- **Who uses it:** Sales teams, sales managers, sales operations, leadership

## Why it matters

Without pipeline visibility, sales managers can't track individual progress, revenue predictions become inaccurate, and business decisions suffer. Well-managed pipelines enable managers to identify problems early, intervene on stalled deals, and provide support where needed. Leadership also receives trustworthy revenue forecasts.

## How it works

Sales Pipeline begins by defining process stages. Typical pipelines divide into 5-8 stages like initial contact, needs analysis, proposal, negotiation, and closing. Each stage defines required activities and advancement criteria.

Sales reps advance deals between stages based on [sales methodology](Sales-Methodology.md), setting closing probability at each stage—10% at initial contact, 30% after needs analysis, 60% after proposing, for example.

Each pipeline deal records customer name, amount, expected close date, and stage. Managers view real-time status on dashboards.

Regular pipeline reviews enable managers to support stalled deals, evaluate low-probability deals, and optimize resource allocation.

## Real-world use cases

**Weekly Sales Meetings** - Managers review pipeline progress with each rep, identifying deals needing support.

**Monthly Revenue Forecasting** - Calculate forecasted revenue from deal amounts and closing probabilities.

**Sales Resource Allocation** - Identify regions or segments needing additional sales staff based on pipeline size.

**Deal Support** - When large deals stall, managers meet with customers to remove barriers.

## Benefits and considerations

Well-managed pipelines dramatically improve revenue forecast accuracy and enable effective manager support. However, preventing salespeople from inflating amounts or overstating probabilities requires regular quality checks and manager approval. Continuing to delete old deals and replenish with new ones maintains pipeline health.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: How many pipeline stages are ideal?**
A: 5-8 stages are typical, adjusting based on industry and sales process length. Too many stages create management complexity.

**Q: How should closing probabilities be set?**
A: Base on historical data—calculate actual closing rates for deals passing through each stage and use those rates as closing probabilities.
